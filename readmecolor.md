# 🎨 FinGPT: Open-Source Financial Large Language Models 🚀

Welcome to the colorful world of FinGPT! This document will guide you through the core ideas, architecture, and usage of this exciting open-source project.

## 💡 Core Idea Behind FinGPT

FinGPT was born out of the need for an accessible, adaptable, and powerful language model specifically designed for the financial domain. Here's what makes FinGPT special:

*   **🌟 Open-Source Spirit**: Unlike proprietary models such as BloombergGPT, FinGPT is open-source. This democratizes access to advanced financial AI, fostering innovation and collaboration within the community. We believe that financial intelligence should be available to everyone, not locked behind expensive licenses.
*   **⚙️ Lightweight Adaptation**: The financial world is incredibly dynamic. Market conditions, regulations, and news change rapidly. Retraining a massive LLM like BloombergGPT frequently is prohibitively expensive (estimated around **$3M per full retrain**!). FinGPT is designed for **lightweight adaptation**. This means it can be fine-tuned with new data quickly and cost-effectively (less than **$300 per fine-tuning**), ensuring the model stays current and relevant.
*   **🤝 Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)**: This is a key differentiator and the "secret sauce" 🌶️. RLHF allows FinGPT to learn individual preferences, such as risk aversion levels, investment habits, and personalized financial advice. This capability, crucial for applications like robo-advisors, is notably missing in models like BloombergGPT.
*   **🌊 Democratizing Internet-Scale Financial Data**: FinGPT aims to make vast amounts of financial data usable for LLMs. It focuses on creating automatic data curation pipelines to ensure timely updates (e.g., monthly or weekly) by leveraging the best available open-source LLMs and data sources, rather than relying on privileged access.

In essence, FinGPT strives to provide a transparent, adaptable, and continuously improving financial language model that can be tailored to specific needs without breaking the bank! 🏦➡️💰

## 🏗️ FinGPT Ecosystem: The Architecture

FinGPT isn't just a single model; it's a full-stack framework designed to handle the complexities of financial data and applications. It's structured in five key layers:

1.  **📊 Data Source Layer**:
    *   **Objective**: Ensures comprehensive market coverage and captures real-time information. Finance is time-sensitive, so this layer is crucial!
    *   **Analogy**: Think of this as the global network of reporters and data feeds constantly supplying fresh information.

2.  **🛠️ Data Engineering Layer**:
    *   **Objective**: Processes real-time Natural Language Processing (NLP) data. It's built to handle the high temporal sensitivity and often low signal-to-noise ratio found in financial data.
    *   **Analogy**: These are the editors and fact-checkers who clean, structure, and prepare the raw information for analysis.

3.  **🧠 LLMs (Large Language Models) Layer**:
    *   **Objective**: This is where the magic happens! It focuses on various fine-tuning methodologies (like LoRA - Low-Rank Adaptation) to adapt pre-trained LLMs to the financial domain. This approach mitigates the highly dynamic nature of financial data, ensuring the model's ongoing relevance and accuracy without full, costly retraining.
    *   **Analogy**: These are the expert analysts who take the processed information and use their specialized knowledge (fine-tuned models) to understand it deeply.

4.  **🎯 Task Layer**:
    *   **Objective**: Executes fundamental financial tasks. These tasks also serve as benchmarks for evaluating performance and comparing different FinLLMs. Examples include sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, question answering, etc.
    *   **Analogy**: These are the specific reports or outputs generated by the analysts, like a sentiment report on a stock or an answer to a financial query.

5.  **🚀 Application Layer**:
    *   **Objective**: Showcases practical applications and live demos, highlighting FinGPT's potential in the financial sector. This includes tools for robo-advisory, sentiment analysis, market prediction, and more.
    *   **Analogy**: These are the final products and services delivered to the end-user, like a personalized investment portfolio or a real-time news analysis dashboard.

## 🛠️ How to Use FinGPT

FinGPT offers a versatile suite of tools and models. Here’s how you can get started and leverage its capabilities:

### Exploring FinGPT Modules 🧩

The FinGPT project is organized into several specialized modules, each catering to different financial NLP tasks. You can find these in the `fingpt/` directory. Key modules include:

*   **[FinGPT-Benchmark](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_Benchmark)**: For instruction tuning and benchmarking LLMs on various financial datasets and tasks.
*   **[FinGPT-Forecaster](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_Forecaster)**: Specifically designed for company news analysis and stock price movement prediction. Try the [demo](https://huggingface.co/spaces/FinGPT/FinGPT-Forecaster)!
*   **[FinGPT-RAG](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_RAG)**: Implements a Retrieval Augmented Generation pipeline, enhancing LLM responses with external knowledge.
*   **[FinGPT-FinancialReportAnalysis](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_FinancialReportAnalysis)**: Uses LLMs to analyze financial reports.
*   **[FinGPT-Sentiment-Analysis (v1, v2, v3)](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt)**: Various versions focusing on sentiment analysis, with v3 showcasing the power of LoRA for fine-tuning on academic datasets and achieving state-of-the-art results.
*   **[FinGPT-MultiAgentsRAG](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_MultiAgentsRAG)**: Explores multi-agent systems combined with RAG for more complex financial analysis.
*   And many more, including modules for low-code development, robo-advisory, and trading applications!

For a quick overview of all modules, check out the `fingpt/readme.md` file.

### Pre-trained Models on HuggingFace 🤗

Many FinGPT models, including multi-task financial LLMs and specialized ones like sentiment analyzers and forecasters, are readily available on the [FinGPT HuggingFace page](https://huggingface.co/FinGPT).

You can easily load these models for inference or further fine-tuning. For example:
*   `FinGPT/fingpt-mt_llama2-7b_lora` (Multi-Task Llama2-7b)
*   `FinGPT/fingpt-sentiment_llama2-13b_lora` (Sentiment Analysis Llama2-13b)
*   `FinGPT/fingpt-forecaster_dow30_llama2-7b_lora` (Stock Forecaster Llama2-7b)

### Training and Fine-tuning 🏋️‍♀️

FinGPT emphasizes ease of adaptation. You'll find numerous Jupyter notebooks and scripts within each module to help you:

*   **Prepare your data**: See examples in `FinGPT_Benchmark/data/` or `FinGPT_Sentiment_Analysis_v3/data/`.
*   **Fine-tune models using LoRA**: This is a core strength of FinGPT, allowing efficient fine-tuning on consumer hardware (like a single RTX 3090).
    *   Example for Sentiment Analysis with Llama2-13B (8-bit): `fingpt/FinGPT_Sentiment_Analysis_v3/training_8bit/train_Llama2_13B.ipynb`
    *   Example for ChatGLM2-6B: `FinGPT_Training_LoRA_with_ChatGLM2_6B_for_Beginners.ipynb`
*   **Run benchmarks**: Evaluate model performance on standard financial datasets.
    *   Example: `fingpt/FinGPT_Sentiment_Analysis_v3/benchmark/benchmarks.ipynb`

## 🔧 How FinGPT is Built

FinGPT's effectiveness and adaptability stem from its careful selection of underlying technologies and methodologies:

*   **🧱 Foundation of Open-Source Base Models**: FinGPT leverages powerful, existing open-source Large Language Models (LLMs) as its foundation. This provides a strong starting point for financial specialization. Key base models include:
    *   **Llama-2 (7B, 13B)**: Excels with English-based market data and shows superior fine-tuning results.
    *   **Falcon (7B)**: Offers a good balance of performance and resource efficiency for English data.
    *   **MPT (7B)**: Known for efficient training and stable convergence.
    *   **Bloom (7B1)**: A large open multilingual model, useful for diverse financial contexts.
    *   **ChatGLM2 (6B)**: Particularly strong for Chinese language financial data.
    *   **Qwen (7B)**: Effective for Chinese market data, known for fast response and high accuracy.
    *   **InternLM (7B)**: Another strong contender for Chinese financial NLP.
    The main `README.md` contains a table with benchmark results for these base models on financial sentiment analysis tasks.

*   **🚀 Efficient Fine-tuning with LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation)**:
    A core principle of FinGPT is cost-effective adaptation. Instead of retraining entire multi-billion parameter models, FinGPT extensively uses **LoRA**.
    LoRA allows for fine-tuning by adding a small number of trainable parameters (adapters) to the existing LLM layers. This dramatically reduces:
    *   Computational cost (can be done on consumer GPUs like RTX 3090).
    *   Training time.
    *   Storage requirements for model checkpoints (only the small adapter weights need to be saved).
    This makes it feasible to regularly update FinGPT models with the latest financial data and trends.

*   **📊 Data-Centric Approach & FinNLP**:
    High-quality, well-processed data is paramount for any successful LLM. FinGPT adopts a data-centric approach, facilitated by the **[FinNLP library](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinNLP)**.
    *   **Data Collection**: FinNLP helps in gathering financial data from diverse sources (news APIs, social media, filings, etc.).
    *   **Data Processing**: It provides tools for cleaning, preprocessing, and structuring this data specifically for financial NLP tasks.
    *   **Instruction Tuning Datasets**: FinGPT also curates and utilizes instruction tuning datasets (available on [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/FinGPT)) to teach models how to follow specific financial task instructions (e.g., "What is the sentiment of this news? Choose from positive/neutral/negative.").

*   **🧪 Benchmarking and Evaluation**:
    Continuous improvement requires rigorous evaluation. The **[FinGPT-Benchmark](https://github.com/AI4Finance-Foundation/FinGPT/tree/master/fingpt/FinGPT_Benchmark)** module is dedicated to this. It provides:
    *   Standardized financial datasets (e.g., FPB, FiQA-SA, TFNS, NWGI).
    *   Scripts and notebooks to evaluate models fine-tuned with different strategies (task-specific, multi-task, zero-shot).
    This allows for transparent comparison and tracking of progress.

*   **🤖 RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)**:
    As mentioned in the core ideas, RLHF is a key component planned for enhancing FinGPT's capabilities, especially for applications requiring personalization and alignment with user preferences (e.g., robo-advisors). This involves collecting human feedback on model outputs and using it to further refine the model.

By combining these elements—strong open-source foundations, efficient adaptation techniques, a robust data pipeline, and a commitment to evaluation—FinGPT provides a powerful and evolving platform for financial AI.
